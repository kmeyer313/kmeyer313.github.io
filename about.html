Background

I grew up in a small town west of Chicago surrounded by a wonderful family that takes advantage of every opportunity to get together. In 2005, I moved to Milwaukee where I earned a bachelor's degree in elementary education and in history from Marquette University. Following graduation, I taught 5th grade at a progressive parochial school in Milwaukee where my passion for research-based best practice in education was both fostered and challenged.

After teaching for 5 years, my desire to work in education grew beyond the walls of a school building. I did not enjoy teaching or planning curriculum anymore, but found that my side projects brought me greater fulfillment. On the side I was trying to find and build technological tools that could warehouse school-wide curriculum, that supported planning, and that simplified communication and collaboration.

At the time I didn’t exactly know what arena my side projects fell into until I stumbled upon Instructure, an educational software company. My school’s technology integration specialist had asked me to look into Instructure’s learner management system, Canvas, for use at our school. I quickly found myself on their Careers page scanning job opportunities, wondering what skills I had that could be useful to them. I had no idea what a software engineer was, but as I learned, I realized they had the skills I needed to make better technological tools in education.

Soon after I found out about Instructure, I learned about coding bootcamps. Dev Bootcamp was most appealing to me as it focuses not only on the technical skills I want to develop, but also on metacognitive skills to learn new skills after bootcamp and it fosters growth in emotional intelligence. It became very clear what direction I wanted to take next to develop the skills to make beautiful and meaningful software for education and other fields.
